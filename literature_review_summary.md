# Literature Review Summary: Quantum Corrections to Black Hole Ringdown

## Key Findings

### 1. **Theoretical Framework Already Exists**

**Loop Quantum Gravity (LQG) Predictions:**
- Area quantization: minimum area ~ γ l_P² (γ is Barbero-Immirzi parameter)
- Space has discrete structure at Planck scale
- QNM frequencies should be modified near Planck scale
- Reference: Phys. Rev. D 109, 044044 (2024) - "Quasinormal modes of loop quantum black holes"

**Key Result**: LQG predicts frequency shifts, but primarily for black holes NEAR Planck mass (10^-5 grams), not solar mass BHs!

### 2. **Quantum Horizon Effects**

**Partially Reflective Horizon (Phys. Rev. D 106, 024041):**
- Horizon modeled as quantum membrane with Gaussian fluctuations
- Leads to frequency-dependent reflectivity
- Results in "echoes" after main ringdown
- Modified QNM spectrum

**Implication for our analysis**: Should look for echoes, not just frequency shifts!

### 3. **Actual Experimental Searches**

**LIGO/Virgo Tests:**
- No deviations from GR found so far in GWTC-1, GWTC-2
- Upper limits set on modified gravity parameters
- Frequency shifts < 0.1% ruled out for most events

**Problem**: Our expected effect (l_P/r_s)² ~ 10^-80 is WAY below detection limits

### 4. **Alternative Signatures**

**Nonlinear Effects (arXiv:2306.09974):**
- Frequency doubling in ringdown
- Graviton number statistics
- Could be detectable with quantum-enhanced detectors

**GUP (Generalized Uncertainty Principle):**
- Δx ≥ ħ/(2Δp) + α(l_P²/ħ)Δp
- Prevents infinite compression
- Modifies effective potential

### 5. **What Could Actually Be Detectable?**

**Near-Extremal Black Holes:**
- Corrections amplified near extremality (spin ~ 1)
- But GW150914 has moderate spin (a ~ 0.7)

**Overtone Anomalies:**
- Higher overtones more sensitive to near-horizon physics
- n=1 mode decays faster than n=0
- Ratio f₁/f₀ might deviate from GR

**Time Evolution:**
- Early ringdown (< 2ms) might show transient deviations
- "Quantum burst" before settling to classical QNM
